The Count of Monte Cristo

by: Alexandre Dumas



The story begins in 1815 and ends in 1844 following the fall of the Napoleon's empire. The plot tells of a story of revenge through the eyes of an innocent man named Edmond Dantes, an innocent man who was unjustly accused by his own jealous friends and was thrown in an unforgiving prison called Chateau d'If. There, he learns much wisdom from a sage who was also imprisoned for the same reasons like him. After escaping the prison, Edmond constructs an ingenious plan of revenge against those who wronged him. The moral of the story is that "Revenge does more damage to the avenger than to the avenged." This is one of the best pieces of literary genius tells the story of betrayal survival, faith, revenge and God. The author presents both revenge and redemption as being motivated by love.
